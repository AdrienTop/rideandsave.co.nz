New Zealand's best-selling vehicles

Official NZ fuel consumption from NZTA fleet data, with real-world estimates based on mandatory on-board monitoring of 600,000 vehicles — published by the European Commission.

Where this data comes from

Vehicle rankings and fuel consumption figures come from the NZTA Vehicle Fleet Open Data (2021–2026), which records every vehicle registered in New Zealand. Each model's figure is the average FC_COMBINED value across all new registrations of that model from 2021 to 2026 — covering multiple variants and model years. Models are ranked by total NZ registration count.

How we estimate real-world consumption

Official figures come from standardised conditions, no hills, no AC, no traffic. The European Commission published real-world data from mandatory on-board fuel consumption monitors (OBFCM) fitted to 600,000 vehicles. The gap between lab and road is +23.7% for petrol and +18.1% for diesel.

→ EU Commission OBFCM Report (March 2024)

⚠️ Note: Official figures are from NZTA Vehicle Fleet Open Data (FC_COMBINED), averaged across all registered vehicles of each model. Real-world estimates apply +23.7% for petrol and +18.1% for diesel, based on the EU Commission's mandatory on-board monitoring data (2021, 600,000 vehicles). Your actual consumption will vary with driving style, load, terrain, and climate. EV figures use manufacturer-declared WLTP kWh/100km (no NZTA consumption data available for EVs).
